About

Hi, I am Alex Hung (孔令宇), a ph.D. student in the Computer Science department at UCLA working in Sung Lab where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Kyung Hyun Sung and Prof. Demetri Terzopoulos. Prior to joining UCLA, I was working in Biomedical Image Guidance Lab at CMU as a Master student under prof. John Galeotti. Before graduate study, I received my Bachelor’s degree in Biomedical Engineering from Peking University where I worked with Prof. Tianyu Xie and also minored in Computer Science. In my undergraduate years, I also briefly worked in Penn Image Computing and Science Lab under the supervision of Prof. Paul Yushkevich.

My main research interest is to apply computer vision and machine learning approaches to medical image analysis. In particular, I am interested in developing computer vision algorithms that help downstream algorithms or clinicians with diagnosis or surgeries, as well as incorporation other clinical information into vision-based methods.

Education

  • August 2019-present: Research Assistant, Biomedical Image Guidance Lab (Carnegie Mellon University) , Pittsburgh, USA
    • Accomplished probabilistic reverberation artifact segmentation on ultrasound images under weak and semi supervision with a new probabilistic segmentation network, hierarchical probabilistic U-Net and a physics-based artifact refinement algorithm (submitted to ICRA 2021)
    • Created a directed graph-based algorithm to model per-pixel confidence of ultrasound images, which allowed better compounding, shadow detection, and helped robotic ultrasound systems find the optimal direction to move the probe (accepted at ISBI 2021)
  • May 2020-August 2020: Research Intern, Philips Respironics, Pittsburgh, USA
    • Developed 3D facial reconstruction algorithm where the inputs were selfies and outputs were the 3D faces
    • Built models that predict the vest sizes for patients given their body measurements such as heights and weights
  • December 2016-June 2019: Research Assistant, Tianyu Xie’s Lab (Peking University), Beijing, China
    • Created a stereo matching algorithm based on feature detection and energy minimization
    • Designed 3D endoscopic cameras based on epipolar geometry with the limitation of space on endoscopes and reconstructed the 3D scene
  • July 2018-September 2018: Research Intern, Penn Image Computing & Science Laboratory (University of Pennsylvania), Philadelphia, USA
    • Worked on a 10-week research project to detect and segment neurofibrillary tangles in whole histology slides
    • Completed the detection of tangles using color channel alteration and SE-ResNet based neural network, and reached an accuracy of 97.21%, a sensitivity of 97.40%, a specificity of 97.07%
